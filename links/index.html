Links

These are links to blogs I visit often.

  • Moody Rd: Blog of Moritz Hardt, a research scientist at Google. Mainly about theoretical aspects of machine learning, but the exposition is elementary and the core ideas are quite approachable.
  • Off the convex path: Collections of articles on miscellaneous aspects of optimization methods. Many interesting research directions and open problems. The writings are quite accessible.
  • Joel on Software: More that 1000 articles, covering may aspects of software development.
  • Matt Might’s Blog: Insights about functional programming as well as tons of invaluable career advices for graduate students.
  • Alvaro Videla’s Blog: Distributed system development. RabbitMQ.
  • What’s new: Terry Tao’s blog, mainly about mathematics. Actually I don’t understand most of its contents, but for a few that can be perceived, the writing is just great (e.g. the article about Moser’s entropy compression argument).
  • Computational Complexity: Lance Fortnow and Bill Gasarch’s blog.
