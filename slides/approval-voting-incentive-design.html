Approval Voting and Incentives in Crowdsourcing

Approval Voting and Incentives in Crowdsourcing

ICML 2015

Nihar B. Shah
Dengyong Zhou
Yuval Peres

Introduction

Problems faced with crowdsourcing

  • workers not experts, freeloaders(spammers)
  • No proper incentives mechanisms
  • Bad user interfaces

Approval voting

  • Compare single option vs. multiple options
    approval voting can utilize partial knowledge better
  • Problem faced
    workers may be reluctant to act faithfully without proper incentive mechanisms. For instance, with a naive payment rule which gives equal payment every question the worker answers and do not care the number of options returned, then why do a worker
  • Goal
    Incentivize workers to respond with their true knowledge

Promblem settings

  • Multiple options
  • gold-standard questions for evaluate worker performance
  • final payment based on the evaluated performance
  • Assumption: workers aim to maximize their expected payment
  • non-negative payment

Main results

Which options on earth do we want the worker to select?

  • Assume workers have a belief on which options are probable, e.g. option A may have 50% chance to be the correct one, while option B and option C seem less probable, although one of them may turn out to be the correct one.
  • A Incentive-compatible mechanism should encourage the worker to respond with all options he thinks maybe correct.
  • An impossibility result
    there exists no incentive-compatible payment mechanisms.

Workaround

  • Coarse belief
    The belief on a option is either larger than some constant \(\rho>0\) or zero.
  • observation from psychology
  • Under this assumption, there exists incentive-compatible payment mechanisms.

The proposed method:

Input: payment budget B, workers' answers to goldstandard, coarse belief parameter $\rho$
Output:
    For every question in gold standard:
        For every wrong option selected, payment multiplied by $(1-\rho)$
        if the correct option is not selected, payment becomes zero

The optimality of proposed method

  • Among all incentive-compatible mechanisms, the proposed one gives smallest payment to spammers (i.e. workers returning all options for every queston)
  • Unique under a no-free-lunch requirement
    • no-free-lunch
      if all attempted answers are wrong, then payment should be zero.
    • This is a weak requirement
      no restriction if even one of the answers is correct
    • Surprisingly, the proposed method is the only incentive-compatible mechanism satisfying the no-free-lunch requirement.

Experiments

Settings

  • Goal: to verify the three following things:
    • If faced with approval-voting interface, whether the worker will use it judiciously
    • whether the mechanism improves quality
    • whether works hate the interface or the mechanism, if so, then why?
  • Settings:
    • 2 sets of experiment, 200 workers for each.
    • Each worker is faced with a interface randomly choosen from below:
      • Single-selection interface with additive payments
      • Skip-based single-selection interface with multiplicative payments
      • Approval-voting interface with a fixed payment
      • Approval-voting interface with the proposed payment rule

Result

  • The workers made judicious use of the approval-voting interface whenever made available to them
  • the approval-voting based approach coupled with the proposed mechanism successfully converts a significant fraction of incorrect answers to correct partial information.
  • did not receive any negative feedback about either the approval voting interface or the proposed mechanism.
